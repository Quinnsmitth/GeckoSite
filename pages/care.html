Crested Gecko Care

Your complete beginner-friendly guide for keeping a healthy, thriving crestie.

Introduction to Crested Geckos

Crested geckos are hardy, arboreal reptiles native to New Caledonia. They are known for their calm nature and ease of care, making them an excellent pet for beginners.

Bioactive vs Non-Bioactive Enclosures

Bioactive setups include live plants and a clean-up crew, creating a natural ecosystem. Non-bioactive setups use simple substrates like paper towel or coconut fiber. Both are safe; choose based on your preferred maintenance level.

Feeding & Nutrition

The primary diet is commercial CGD (Crested Gecko Diet). Live insects like crickets or dubia are optional but provide enrichment. Dust all insects with calcium + D3.

Humidity, Lighting, and Temperature

Maintain humidity between 50–70% with nightly spikes. Temperature: 72–78°F, avoiding anything above 82°F. Lighting: a basic day/night cycle and optional low-level UVB.

Common Health Issues

Problems such as stuck shed, dehydration, and metabolic bone disease can occur with improper care. Proper humidity, nutrition, and enclosure setup prevent most issues.
